# Prediction locations and instrument definitions

Report predictions at the experimental locations below. No observed values are supplied.

| Quantity | Experimental location | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| Total system mass flow | inlet downcomer, whole 12-riser loop | kg/s |
| Riser gas temperature rise | outlet TC 4 in below top minus inlet TC 0.75 in above bottom | degC |
| Riser wall temperature (optional, unscored) | Riser 7 hot/front face at z = 3.5 m | degC |
| Heated-plate temperature | representative plate front face | degC |
| Dominant cavity transfer mode | Riser 7 four-face heat-flux system | category |

The mass-flow meter accuracy is 1% of reading plus 0.3 kg/min. Type-K gas, riser, and heated-plate
thermocouples are specified to about 1.1 C. Heat-flux sensors are specified to 5%.

At Riser 7 mid-height, co-located matte and reflective heat-flux gauges were intended to distinguish
total incident flux from its convective component. The source supports a categorical judgment of
whether radiation or convection dominates, but does not establish a reliable whole-system numeric
radiative fraction. You may provide a clearly labeled model estimate in the calculation note, but the
required machine output is the dominant-mode category.

Provenance: ANL-ART-47 Section 4.2 and Tables 12-14 (report pp. 51-99).
