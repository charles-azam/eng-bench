# Air-cooled NSTF geometry

The facility is a half-axial-scale, 19.03-degree sector of an air-cooled reactor cavity cooling
system. A hot steel plate faces a row of 12 vertical steel riser ducts across an air-filled cavity.
Air enters through a downcomer and lower plenum, rises through the heated ducts by buoyancy, enters an
upper plenum, and leaves through two chimney stacks. There are no pumps in the cases studied here.

## Heated cavity and plate

- Cavity height: 6.7 m; width: 1.32 m.
- Baseline hot-plate-face to riser-front-face spacing: 0.7066 m.
- As-built heated plate area: 10.18 m2.
- Heated plate: SAE 1020 steel, 25.4 mm thick.
- North, south, and west cavity walls are insulated; the east wall is heated.

## Riser bank

- 12 ASTM A500 Grade B rectangular steel ducts.
- Outer cross-section per duct: 10 in x 2 in; wall thickness: 0.188 in.
- Approximate inner cross-section: 9.624 in x 1.624 in.
- Approximate internal flow area per duct: 0.0101 m2.
- Approximate hydraulic diameter: 0.0707 m.
- Total duct length: 7.49 m; length within the heated cavity: 6.91 m.
- One 2-in narrow face of each duct faces the heated plate. The two 10-in faces face neighboring
  ducts.

## Flow path

- Inlet downcomer: uninsulated 24-in (0.61 m) diameter duct; equivalent centerline length 184.5 in.
- Inlet plenum interior: 44 in high x 51.75 in wide x about 31.75 in deep.
- Outlet plenum interior: 74 in x 74 in x 64 in. Risers protrude 16 in above its floor.
- Two 24-in chimney entrance ports, 56.5 in above the plenum floor.
- Dual insulated 24-in chimney stacks. Baseline discharge height: 19.6 m.
- Equivalent chimney lengths from the outlet plenum: 826 in vertical and 470 in horizontal.
- Dampers are open and blowers are off.

## Known geometric ambiguities

The exact riser pitch, internal corner radii, and heated-plate envelope are available only in source
drawings. State and propagate any approximations made for them.

(report pp. 27-40). Thermal-duty values from the source scaling tables are deliberately omitted from
